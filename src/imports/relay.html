The Problem

Hold time is dead air.
It doesn't have to be.

Every minute on hold is unproductive for the customer and costly for your operation. When an agent finally connects, they start from zero.

01
The repeat explanation tax
Customers re-explain their issue to every touchpoint — IVR, agent, escalation. Each handoff erases context and burns time. Agents start cold on every call.
02
Hold time is a black hole
13 minutes of average hold time generates zero business value today. Customers disengage, abandonment climbs, and there's no mechanism to capture intent during the wait.
03
Agents inherit chaos
When connection finally happens, agents scramble — account lookup, reason for call, urgency level. The first few minutes of every call are pure administrative overhead.
04
AHT is a blunt lever
Average Handle Time is the core efficiency metric. Most reduction efforts focus on post-connection speed. Relay attacks the pre-connection window nobody else has touched.
How It Works

Four steps. Zero friction.

Relay intercepts hold time the moment it starts — no change to your existing call flow, no new hardware required.

01
Customer calls in
Your existing IVR routes the call as normal. When the customer enters a hold queue, Relay activates automatically via Twilio.
02
Relay takes the hold
Our AI voice agent greets the caller, confirms their reason for calling, and collects structured intake through natural conversation.
03
Brief is generated
Relay compiles a structured handoff brief — issue category, urgency, account details, and sentiment — ready at connection.
04
Agent connects, informed
At connection, the brief surfaces instantly. The agent already knows who they're talking to and why. The call starts at resolution.
